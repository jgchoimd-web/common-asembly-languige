# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

CommonASM is a small compiler project, but it should still be a place where
people can ask questions, propose targets, and make mistakes without being
mocked or pushed away.

Expected behavior:

- Be respectful when reviewing ideas, code, examples, and target requests.
- Explain disagreements with technical reasons and reproducible examples.
- Assume good intent, especially from new contributors.
- Keep discussions focused on CommonASM, compiler behavior, documentation, and
  project maintenance.

Unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, threats, insults, or personal attacks.
- Deliberately derailing issues or pull requests.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Spam, malware, or intentionally harmful code.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces such as issues, pull requests,
discussions, documentation, examples, and any official CommonASM community
channel.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments and issues that violate
this code of conduct. Repeated or serious violations may lead to a block from
the repository.

If you see a problem, open an issue with enough context for maintainers to
understand what happened. Do not include private information in public reports.
